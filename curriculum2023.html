Learning goals

1. Connect the historical context of genetics research to its modern-day practice
2. Evaluate the social and ethical implications of genetics research
3. Analyze how societal norms and structures, along with personal identities, biases, and responsibility, impact the conduct of scientific research

Session 3: Race, Ancestry, Identity, and Genetics Pt 1

Learning objectives

  • Distinguish between race, ethnicity, ancestry, and nationality.
  • Differentiate between social identity and genetic ancestry, and in particular, understand how social identity is (often) discretized, while genetic ancestry is a continuum.
  • Discuss the history of eugenics and medical mistrust among racial/ethnic groups through discussions of historical cases/events, such as the case of Henrietta Lacks.
  • Understand how race/ethnicity becomes biological through the effects of racism and how this is relevant for genetic medicine and research.

Pre-class assignment

  • Watch two videos on Henrietta Lacks
  • Watch two videos on forced sterilization
  • Read an article on carrier screening

Optional readings

  • Read What is ancestry? by Iain Mathieson and Aylwyn Scally

Session 7: Screening and Selection

Learning objectives

  • Understand the role of eugenics in the development of the prenatal genetic testing field.
  • Summarize the current landscape of prenatal genetic testing, carrier screening, embryo selection, and gene editing.
  • Extrapolate the current and future implications of the currently available technologies with a focus on reproductive choice and disability rights.
  • Evaluate how ethics can help guide society towards ethical application of these technologies.

Session 8: Identification and Privacy

Learning objectives

  • Analyze the role that government, private and public databases play in the application of forensic genetics in the criminal justice system globally.
  • Examine risks and benefits of identification and privacy from private and public entities.

Pre-class assignment

    Readings: You will be divided into 2 groups, and this will be done via email. You will be given two data policies from a variety of different databases.
    Possible Data policies
  • 23andMe: 23andMe is a for-profit company that offers direct-to-consumer genetic testing services to individuals, providing information about their ancestry and health traits. 23andMe also collaborates with pharmaceutical companies to utilize its extensive genetic database for research and development purposes.
  • UK Biobank: UK Biobank is a large-scale research project that collects and stores biological samples and health data from half a million individuals, working in partnership with the British National Health Service.
  • Ancestry: Ancestry is a for-profit company that enables individuals to discover and explore their family history by providing access to vast collections of historical records, direct-to-consumer DNA testing services, and an extensive network of family trees.
  • H3Africa: H3Africa is a collaborative academic initiative that collects and analyzes genomic data from diverse African populations. H3Africa strongly emphasizes community engagement as a fundamental principle of their work, and specifically seeks to empower local scientists and institutions.
    • While reading through your data policies, ask yourself the following questions:
  • What type of data will be collected & generated?
  • What types of analyses / questions will be asked with the data?
  • Who (else) has access to the data?
  • How does a participant withdraw permission?
  • How well were the above questions communicated?
